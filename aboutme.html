About me

I was born in Maracaibo, Venezuela and I immigrated to Katy, Texas in 2007 with my parents. At first, I considered the move to the U.S. the worst thing that could have happened to me, but looking back, I see how it truly pushed me to be the driven person I am today. I was put into a bilingual program that allowed me to embrace the culture I knew and loved, and the culture I was new to. Within my first year of school, I could read, write, and speak fluent English as well as Spanish. Every year from then on, I felt the need to further improve myself through hard work. I spent the majority of my time on school, volunteering, and volleyball.

My junior year of high school I was honored by the National Hispanic Recognition Program as an academically talented Hispanic high school student by scoring in the top 2.75% of Hispanics in the nation who took the PSAT. Furthermore, I received the AP Scholar with Distinction Award from the College Board my senior year of high school. My hard work payed off when I was accepted into the Engineering program at Texas A&M University. I spent my freshman year at A&M fervently learning in order to enter into my dream major: Computer Science.

Now, as a sophomore, I am a part of the Computer Science major and I have become more involved with the university in order to find new challenges. I joined the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers where I am a member of the Technical Affairs committee. This has introduced me to website development using HTML and CSS. Throught this year I will also be working on our Point Sheet Automation System. I was also selected as a top engineering student into the STEM to Stocks program, which is designed to introduce STEM majors to business and networking.
